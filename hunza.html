Hunza Valley

Nestled in the heart of the Karakoram mountains in Gilgit-Baltistan, Hunza Valley is one of Pakistan's most breathtaking destinations. With snow-capped peaks, vivid turquoise lakes, ancient forts, and warm hospitality, Hunza offers an experience unlike any other.

Top Attractions

Rakaposhi Viewpoint

Rakaposhi, standing at 7,788 metres, is the 27th highest mountain in the world and one of Pakistan's most iconic peaks. The classic viewpoint along the Karakoram Highway near Minapin village gives visitors an unobstructed, jaw-dropping panorama of the entire south face of the mountain. On clear mornings the summit reflects golden light over the glacier below, making it a favourite spot for photographers and mountaineers.

  • Best Time to Visit: April to October
  • Nearest Town: Minapin, Nagar District
  • Activity: Sightseeing, Photography, Trekking
  • Tip: Arrive before 9 AM for cloud-free views.

Attabad Lake

Attabad Lake was formed in January 2010 after a massive landslide blocked the Hunza River, creating a natural reservoir approximately 21 kilometres long. Today it is celebrated for its vivid turquoise water that contrasts sharply with the rugged brown mountains surrounding it. Visitors can enjoy boat rides, jet skiing, and kayaking on the lake.

  • Best Time to Visit: May to September
  • Location: Gojal Valley, Upper Hunza
  • Activity: Boating, Jet Skiing, Photography
  • Tip: Hire a local wooden boat for the best experience.

Baltit Fort

Baltit Fort is a 700-year-old royal residence perched dramatically above the town of Karimabad and overlooking the entire Hunza Valley. Once the home of the Mirs (rulers) of Hunza, it was renovated with support from the Aga Khan Trust for Culture and is now a UNESCO World Heritage candidate site. The fort houses a museum of royal artefacts and centuries-old household items.

  • Best Time to Visit: April to November
  • Location: Karimabad, Hunza
  • Activity: Heritage Tour, Museum, Photography
  • Tip: Combine with Altit Fort for a full day of history.

Travel Tips for Hunza

  • How to Get There: Fly to Gilgit Airport from Islamabad, then drive 2 hours to Karimabad via KKH.
  • Best Season: Spring (April-May) for cherry blossoms; Summer (June-August) for trekking.
  • Local Food: Try Chapshuro, Diram Phiti, and fresh glacier water.
  • Altitude: Karimabad sits at 2,438 m - allow 1 day to acclimatize.
  • Currency: Carry cash (PKR); ATMs are limited in remote areas.
